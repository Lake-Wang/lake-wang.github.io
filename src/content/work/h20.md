## Overview

As a Data Scientist Intern on NBCUniversal's Forecasting team, I built a production hierarchical forecasting framework that now supports pricing, inventory, and executive reporting across Finance and Ad Sales. The system spans 11 digital platforms and had to be robust to the volatility and trend shifts characteristic of streaming media data.

## Technical Approach

The framework was built on Spark/SQL for scalability across platforms and data volumes. Key engineering decisions included:

- **Detrending and outlier filters** to isolate signal from media-specific noise (e.g., content release spikes, seasonal patterns)
- **Feature engineering** capturing platform-level and cross-platform dynamics
- **Guardrails** to keep forecasts within business-meaningful bounds during anomalous periods

The hierarchical structure ensures consistency across aggregation levels — individual platform forecasts reconcile with portfolio-level totals, which is essential for financial planning workflows.

## Results

The framework improved forecast error from 27% MAPE to 13%, and R² from 0.72 to 0.86. More importantly, it was adopted directly into the planning workflows used by Finance and Ad Sales — the real benchmark for success in a forecasting role isn't the metric, it's whether the business trusts the output enough to plan around it.

## Collaboration

Beyond the technical work, this project required close collaboration with data engineers, analysts, and business stakeholders across teams — translating between model behavior and business requirements in both directions. It made clear that forecasting in production is as much a communication and trust problem as it is a modeling one.
